About the Service

This service enables community growers and small-scale farmers to browse available seeds and seedlings and submit requests online for food production and sustainable gardening.

All requests are submitted digitally through the app for convenience and transparency.

Once approved, seeds or seedlings must be claimed in person at a designated DA office, partner nursery, seed center, or barangay distribution site, as indicated in the approval notice.

This approach supports proper distribution, basic orientation on planting practices, and strengthens community-led food security efforts.

Who may request

This service is intended for community growers and small-scale agricultural contributors who aim to support local food production and sustainability.

Eligible requestors include:

  • Individual small-scale farmers and backyard growers
  • Community gardeners and urban farming advocates
  • Neighborhood or barangay-based gardening groups
  • Student groups and school-led food production initiatives
  • Informal farming collectives and seed-sharing communities
  • Individuals growing crops for household consumption, community feeding programs, or local sharing initiatives

Requests must support food security, sustainable agriculture, or community livelihood, and may not be used for large-scale commercial resale.
